Facts About Jim Rohn

Emanuel James Rohn (September 17, 1930 – December 5, 2009) professionally known as Jim Rohn, was an American entrepreneur, author and motivational speaker.

  • Home: Jim Rohn was a farm boy from Caldwell, Idaho.where he grew up, being the only child.
  • College: Jim left college after just one year and started his professional life by working as a human resource manager for department store Sears.
  • In 1955, Jim met a man by the name John Earl Shoaff in a lecture. He joined Shoaff's direct selling business AbundaVita as a distributor. .
  • In 1957, Rohn resigned his distributorship with AbundaVita and joined Nutri-Bio. The company's founders, including Shoaff, started to mentor him.
    After this mentorship, Rohn built one of the largest organizations in the company. In 1960 when Nutri-Bio expanded into Canada, Rohn was selected
    as a vice president for the organization.
  • Throughout the 1970s, Rohn conducted a number of seminars for Standard Oil.He also participated in a personal development business called
    "Adventures in Achievement" worldwide.
  • Rohn was the recipient of the 1985 National Speakers Association CPAE Award for excellence in speaking.
  • He is also the author of 17 different written, audio, and video media, including "The Power of Ambition", "Take Charge of Your Life", and "The Day
    That Turns Your Life Around". Many of his speeches are now available for free on social media platforms like YouTube and Instagram.
  • Jim Rohn died of pulmonary fibrosis on December 5, 2009. He is buried in Forest Lawn Memorial Park in Glendale, California.

“Success is neither magical nor mysterious. Success is the natural consequence of consistently applying the basic fundamentals.” Jim Rohn.
